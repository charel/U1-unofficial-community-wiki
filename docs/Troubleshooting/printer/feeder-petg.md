# Creaking and clog errors when printing PETG
## Description
On a stock U1, with the spools loaded on the side and the feeders installed like the manual instructs, printing with PETG can lead to clog detection and cause creaking. 
## Cause
The PETG rubs against the metal inlet rings on the feeders, especially the bottom spools, causing them to creak and eventually to underextrude and raise a clog detection error. 
## Solutions
Print [this PTFE adapter for the feeders](https://makerworld.com/en/models/2176077-snapmaker-u1-ptfe-adapter-m6-m10-ecas04) and insert a few cm/inches of PTFE. I recommend using M10 or ECAS04 couplings, as the M6 coupling doesn't allow the tube to go through the coupling and "connect" to the feeder inlet ring. 
Alternatively, one can also print [these TPU PTFE adapters](https://makerworld.com/en/models/2156716-snapmaker-u1-ptfe-adapter-feeder).
